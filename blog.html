    What is AI and machine learning?

      Artificial intelligence (AI) makes it possible for machines to learn from experience, adjust to new inputs and perform human-like tasks. Most AI examples that you hear about today – from chess-playing computers to self-driving cars – rely heavily on deep learning and natural language processing. Using these technologies, computers can be trained to accomplish specific tasks by processing large amounts of data and recognizing patterns in the data. (Insights, 2020) Artificial intelligence (AI) is wide-ranging branch of computer science concerned with building smart machines capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence. (What is Artificial Intelligence? How Does AI Work? | Built In, 2020) When it comes to AI, there are a large number of different opinions and definitions as mention above. However, by this mean artificial intelligence (AI) unlike natural intelligent, is a machine capable of performing tasks and solve problems that human considers intelligence.

      Machine learning is a branch of artificial intelligence (AI) focused on building applications that learn from data and improve their accuracy over time without being programmed to do so. (What is Machine Learning?, 2020) Therefore, machine learning could be considered as a subset of AI as it helps AI to improve intelligence through learning to predict or decide without the human guideline. However, teaching new abilities for AI can be quite different from humans as AI can only learn through experiences. Nowadays, with a large variaty of AI that has been developed human have separated them into four different categories:

    • Reactive Machines

      Is the oldest form of AI and has a limited capability. Which doesn't have the ability to learn from their previous action.

      Can be understood as the upgrade version of reactive machines as it can store historical data to make decisions. Nowadays, this AI is mainly used by applications that require AI assistant.

      This type of AI is still in development. As the next level of AI, It can learn by analyzing human behavior base on their needs and emotions

    • Self-aware

      Is the final stage of AI development, however it still just a hypothesis in science fiction. This AI can replicate human means they can understand emotions, needs, beliefs, and potentially desires.

    Cloud computing

    What is Cloud computing?

      Cloud computing is the on-demand delivery of IT resources over the Internet with pay-as-you-go pricing. Instead of buying, owning, and maintaining physical data centers and servers, you can access technology services, such as computing power, storage, and databases, on an as-needed basis from a cloud provider like Amazon Web Services (AWS). (What is Cloud Computing, 2020) By using cloud technology, companies and developers can both save time and money for owning facilities and maintenance for hardware and software. Cloud computing also provides a better service as users can easily data back up and disaster recovery if the event data loss does occur. There are three main providers in the market which is AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.

      AWS: own by AWS Inc. launch in 2006 provides in 25 geographical regions and 78 Availability Zones

      Azure: own by Microsoft launch in 2010 provides in 54 geographical regions and 140 Availability Zones

      Google cloud: own by Google launch in 2008 provides in 21 geographical regions and 61 Availability Zones. (Chand, 2020)

    Types of cloud services:

      infrastructure-as-a-service (IaaS): The most basic category of cloud computing types, IaaS lets you rent IT infrastructure - servers and virtual machines, storage, networks, and operating systems - from a cloud provider on a pay-as-you-go basis.

      Platform-as-a-Service (PaaS): that gives developers the tools to build and host web applications. PaaS is designed to give users access to the components they require to quickly develop and operate web or mobile applications over the Internet, without worrying about setting up or managing the underlying infrastructure of servers, storage, networks, and databases.

      Software-as-a-Service (SaaS): which is used for web-based applications. SaaS is a method for delivering software applications over the Internet where cloud providers host and manage the software applications making it easier to have the same application on all of your devices at once by accessing it in the cloud. (News, 2020)
